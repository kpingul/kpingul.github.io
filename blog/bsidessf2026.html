BSides San Francisco: Infosec Conference 2026

BSidesSF 2026 was my fourth year at the conference. The theme, "The Musical," was a perfect backdrop for the discussions on the orchestration required to manage today’s security challenges. This year, the focus was on the technical reality of securing autonomous agents and handling the scale of modern production.

One of the talks I attended was about the attack surface of CLI agents like Gemini, Claude, and Codex. The presenters looked at how systemic flaws, including command injection and workspace escapes, can lead to cross-process RCE. It is a strong argument for prioritizing sandboxing and verifiability as these agents gain more autonomy in our workflows.

Another technical highlight was the deep dive into CVE-2026-25253, an actual one-click RCE chain in OpenClaw. The presentation dissected how a configuration logic vulnerability could be combined with Cross-Site WebSocket Hijacking to bypass network defenses, sandboxing, and user approval guardrails to take over an AI assistant. Seeing such a detailed breakdown of this vulnerability proved that simple user prompts or basic isolation are not enough to secure agentic platforms.

There was also a lot of discussion about the scale of the npm supply chain. With over 140,000 updates happening every day, manual review is impossible. We are seeing a move toward using automated orchestration to maintain supply chain integrity without breaking the speed of development.

Even with the focus on automation, the human element remains the most important part of the event. One session reframed security history as a series of necessary pivots. The takeaway was that maturity is about building enough scaffolding so that we aren't forced into total improvisation during the next crisis.

BSidesSF 2026 was a solid reminder that as our systems become more autonomous, our community needs to stay even more synchronized. Sharing notes on everything from sandboxing autonomous agents to orchestrating supply chain integrity is what keeps the industry moving forward.

A big thank you to the BSidesSF staff, volunteers, and presenters for putting this all together. I am already looking forward to Year 5.
