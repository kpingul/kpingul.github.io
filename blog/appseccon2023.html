AppSecCon: Application Security Conference

I'm writing to share my first time experience attending AppSecCon 2023, a virtual application security conference.

What is AppSecCon?

AppSecCon,organized by The Purple Book Club, is a two-day event that pulls together a dynamic group of application security professionals, experts, and enthusiasts. The conference covers a broad range of AppSec topics from software security, application security fundamentals, threat modeling to AI in application security and more.

Highlights

AppSecCon kicked off with an opening keynote that turned the spotlight on the importance of software security at the board level. The sessions that followed were a mix of everything from AppSec 101, the role of AI in AppSec, to decoding industry buzzwords like ASOC, ASPM, RBVM, and UVM.

The conference touched on issues like supply chain security and DevSecOps best practices, and even future-facing topics like building a resilient AppSec program. Management-oriented sessions got down to AppSec governance and making a business case for AppSec, with a nod to the role of automation in bridging talent gaps.

The second day put the focus on the journey to AppSec maturity, with discussions on topics such as CISO priorities in software security, the state of AppSecOps in 2023, and the attributes of a modern AppSec maturity model.

Summary

AppSecCon was a blast, packed with enlightening sessions that highlighted the importance and ever-evolving nature of application security. If you're into AppSec, you should definitely mark your calendar for next year. Lastly, a huge shout out to The Purple Book Club, the hosts, presenters, and everyone involved in making this such an awesome event.
